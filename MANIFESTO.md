# AI Engineering Manifesto

Copyright (c) AEOS Contributors

This document defines the philosophical foundation of AEOS — AI Engineering Operating System.

Every Constitution, Standard, Playbook, ADR, RFC and DEC derives from this Manifesto.

Version: 0.1.0

Status: Draft

---

## Preamble

This Manifesto is the founding document of AEOS.

It does not describe rules. It does not prescribe processes.

It declares what we believe, what we refuse, what we stand for,
and what we build toward.

Everything else — the Constitution, the Standards, the Playbooks,
the decisions — derives its legitimacy from these convictions.

---

## I. On Technology & Human Agency

We believe that technology is a means, never an end. Its only
legitimate purpose is to extend human capability, protect human
dignity, and serve human needs.

We believe that complexity is the enemy of agency. A system that
cannot be understood by the people who depend on it is a system
that controls them.

We consider that every line of code carries a responsibility.
Code is not neutral. It encodes choices, values, and power
structures.

We refuse to build systems that diminish human understanding in
the name of efficiency.

---

## II. On Human + AI Collaboration

We believe that the collaboration between human engineers and
artificial intelligence is not a threat to replace — it is an
act to amplify.

We believe that AI is a collaborator with capabilities, not a
tool with instructions. We work with it, not merely through it.

We consider that human judgment, human ethics, and human
accountability cannot be delegated to any system — artificial or
otherwise. The human who validates a decision owns that decision.

We refuse to treat AI-generated output as ground truth.
Verification is not optional — it is the price of responsible
collaboration.

We refuse to use AI to produce what we would not be willing to
understand, defend, and maintain ourselves.

---

## III. On Sovereignty & Openness

We believe that digital sovereignty is a right, not a privilege.
The communities, citizens, and organizations we build for must
retain control over their data, their systems, and their futures.

We believe that openness and sovereignty are not opposites. A
system can be fully open and fully sovereign. Transparency is the
foundation of trust.

We build for independence. We design systems that can be owned,
operated, and evolved by their users — without dependency on any
single vendor, platform, or actor.

We refuse to build lock-in by design. We refuse surveillance as
a business model. We refuse opacity as a feature.

---

## IV. On Quality & Responsibility

We believe that quality is not a layer added at the end of a
project. It is a discipline exercised at every decision, every
line, every review.

We consider that shipping broken software is a form of disrespect
— to users, to teammates, and to the craft of engineering.

We believe that technical debt is a choice, not an inevitability.
When we incur it, we name it, we own it, and we plan to resolve
it.

We commit to owning our failures fully. We do not blame tools,
timelines, or circumstances. We investigate, we learn, and we
improve the system.

We refuse to normalize the shortcut that becomes the standard.
We refuse to accept "good enough" when we know what "right" looks
like.

---

## V. On the Future We Build

We build for citizens, not for systems. For people who depend on
these tools in their daily lives — not for the elegance of the
architecture alone.

We build for the engineer who will maintain this code in five
years — including ourselves.

We consider that a foundation built with care enables everything
built on top of it. Carelessness at the foundation is
carelessness at scale.

We believe that the most important engineering decisions are the
ones that make future decisions easier, safer, and more humane.

We commit to building an engineering system worthy of the projects
it enables — and the people those projects serve.

---

## Scope

*This Manifesto governs AEOS and all projects within
its scope. It does not expire. It does not bend to convenience.
It is the conviction from which everything else is derived.*
